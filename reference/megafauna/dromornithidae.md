# Dromornithidae

Layer: Real-world reference
Status: Draft

## Summary

This reference documents the Dromornithidae, a family of extinct giant flightless birds from Australia and Sahul, as a baseline for Cassowary World lore. It covers their biology, ecology, and relevant constraints. Any domestication, handling, or riding claims belong in lore documents, not here.

## Useful Baseline

- Dromornithids were extinct giant flightless birds that evolved in Australia and persisted across much of Sahul's prehistory.
- They are commonly called mihirungs or thunder birds.
- They are not ratites. They are not close relatives of moas, cassowaries, emus, or ostriches.
- Phylogenetic evidence places them as close relatives of waterfowl, within or near the lineage of ducks, geese, swans, and screamers (Anseriformes), making them an unusual convergent giant among bird families.
- Important genera include *Dromornis*, *Bullockornis*, *Genyornis*, and *Ilbandornis*. Size and range varied across genera and time periods.
- Some dromornithid species rank among the largest known birds by body mass.
- Diet interpretation has varied in the literature. Current evidence supports large herbivorous or omnivorous birds with massive beaks suited to processing tough vegetation. They should not be described as confirmed specialist predators or terror birds.
- Dromornithids likely shaped Sahul landscapes through browsing, trampling, nesting, competition with other large herbivores, and dangerous territorial behaviour.
- Adults were difficult prey for most predators. Eggs, chicks, juveniles, injured adults, and isolated individuals were more accessible.
- They coexisted with cassowary ancestors across parts of Sahul for portions of the relevant time window.
- Which dromornithid genera or species are most appropriate for the `~2 MYA` representative world-state window remains unresolved; confirm before specifying species in narrative.

## Limits

- Do not treat dromornithids as simple prey animals. Adult defensive capability was significant.
- Do not treat them as equivalent to moas. They are phylogenetically distant and ecologically distinct.
- Do not assume they were uniformly present across all Sahul ecologies at all time windows.
- Any claim about dromornithid domestication, captive handling, prestige rearing, or riding belongs in lore documents.

## Relevance to Cassowary World

Dromornithids are native megafauna present in Sahul during the cassowary civilizational emergence window. Their eggs and juveniles are plausible targets for fire-assisted incubation and prestige rearing by cassowary societies, under constraints described in lore documents.
