# A Brief History of Birds

## Summary

Landing page for the reference section on bird evolutionary history. This section stores real-world evolutionary background for birds across deep time, with particular focus on the K-Pg boundary and the emergence of living bird lineages. It provides constraints for any lore that depends on which bird groups existed, survived, or diversified at specific points in geological time.

## Core Reality

- Birds (Aves) evolved from theropod dinosaurs within the Mesozoic. All living birds belong to Neornithes.
- The K-Pg mass extinction (~66 million years ago) eliminated non-avian dinosaurs and the majority of bird lineages then present.
- Living birds descend from neornithine lineages that survived the K-Pg boundary.
- Surviving lineages diversified substantially during the Paleogene and Cenozoic; most modern bird families are outcomes of post-K-Pg radiation.
- Evidence for the timing and sequence of bird diversification comes from two distinct sources: the fossil record and molecular phylogenetics. These can imply different divergence timings and must be kept distinct.

## Constraints

- Fossil evidence and molecular evidence must be distinguished. Molecular divergence dates are estimates dependent on clock models and calibration choices; fossil dates mark confirmed presence, not first appearance.
- Earliest known fossils must not be treated as first evolutionary appearance. Absence of fossil evidence does not establish absence of lineage.
- Living bird families must not be projected backwards unchanged into deep time. A family's modern form is not equivalent to any ancestor that shared its broader lineage.
- Uncertainty must be preserved where lineages, dates, or relationships are contested. Many deep relationships among living birds remain unresolved.
- This section must not generate Cassowary World outcomes directly. It provides constraints that lore documents may use; it does not contain Cassowary World inventions, institutions, or speculative futures.

## System Implications

- Any lore that depends on which birds were present at a specific time must first establish what the reference record supports for that period.
- Claims about bird ancestry, survivorship, or family origins require tracing through the constraints in this section before being treated as settled.
- Apparent simplicity in "bird" categories conceals substantial taxonomic and temporal complexity; lore must not flatten this complexity without flagging it.

## Known Variability

- The fossil record for birds is uneven across regions and time periods; some lineages are documented only through fragmentary remains.
- Molecular clock estimates for early bird divergences vary substantially across studies depending on calibration choices and which gene regions are used.
- The relationships among major Neoaves lineages remain contested, with different analytical methods sometimes yielding conflicting topologies.
