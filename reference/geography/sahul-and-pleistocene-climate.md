## Mechanisms

- Sea-level change alters geography by exposing or flooding shallow continental shelves.
- Glacial cycles change movement routes because land bridges, coastlines, wetlands, and chokepoints shift between phases.
- Elevation and orographic rainfall create local refugia that can remain wetter than surrounding lowlands.
- Rainforest and orchard-suitable zones contract, fragment, or reconnect as rainfall and temperature change.
- Climate variation affects settlement viability by changing where food forests, insect systems, water, and safe travel corridors remain reliable.
