# Aphid-Ant Relationships

Layer: Real-world reference
Status: Draft

## Summary

This reference explains the real-world baseline for aphid-ant relationships used by Cassowary World orchard and honeypot ant systems.

## Useful Baseline

- Aphids feed on plant sap.
- Aphids excrete sugar-rich honeydew.
- Some ants collect honeydew from aphids.
- Some ants protect aphids from predators or competitors as part of a mutualistic relationship.
- Aphid-ant relationships can affect plant health when aphid populations become too dense.

## Relevance to Cassowary World

Cassowary World does not invent aphids, honeydew, or ant-aphid mutualism. Its lore begins when cassowaries stabilize orchard conditions and aphid populations to make honeydew flow more predictable for managed honeypot ant colonies.
