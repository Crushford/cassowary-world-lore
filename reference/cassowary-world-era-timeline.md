# Cassowary World Era Timeline

## Summary

This document anchors the named Cassowary World eras to approximate calendar dates in real-world geological time. It exists because every restructured era doc since PR #13 — `world-state-orchard-era.md`, `world-state-kati-thunda-ant-revolution.md`, `world-state-logistics-stabilisation-era.md`, `world-state-collapse-era.md` — carries an explicit Open Question asking for absolute calendar mapping. Without those answers, every cross-doc claim about sequence, gap duration, and relative timing remains qualitative.

Two hard anchors fix the absolute timeline. The wing-claw divergence onset is approximately 12 million years ago (per `lore/divergences/cassowary-wing-claws-claw-development-era.md`), defining the start of the Claw Development Era. The Protohistoric Expansion Era is approximately 2 million years ago in the Early Pleistocene at a representative glacial maximum (per `reference/world-state-protohistoric-expansion-era.md`). Between these two anchors, roughly 10 million years of biological, behavioural, and civilisational development must distribute across the named eras.

The distribution is heavily uneven. The biological eras (Claw Development, Fire and Extended Development, Ecological Management) span deep time on the order of millions of years each — the timescale required for the cassowary forelimb manipulation pathway, action-language emergence, fire use, and stable ecological partnerships to evolve. The civilisational eras (Orchard, Kati Thunda, Protohistoric Expansion, Early History, Collapse, WTA) compress into a few hundred thousand years near the end of the span — the timescale on which institutional change, storage revolutions, regional expansion, collapse, and reform can plausibly occur.

The ranges given in this document are deliberately wide where evidence is sparse and narrower where multiple biological, ecological, and institutional constraints converge. They are tentative and subject to revision as Cassowary World canon develops. Their purpose is to make cross-doc consistency checks possible, not to commit to exact dates. Where a specific year-count appears, it is illustrative; where a range appears, the range is the canonical claim.

## Core Reality

### Hard anchors

Only two dates are treated as anchor points. All other era boundaries are calibrated against them.

- **Claw Development Era onset: ~12 MYA.** Source: `lore/divergences/cassowary-wing-claws-claw-development-era.md`. This is when rising predator pressure selects for stronger functional claws on cassowary chick vestigial wings. The anchor is approximate; uncertainty is on the order of ±2 million years. The divergence is positioned in the Late Miocene, when palaeontological evidence supports diversifying marsupial-predator pressure across Sahul.
- **Protohistoric Expansion Era canonical snapshot: ~2 MYA.** Source: `reference/world-state-protohistoric-expansion-era.md`, the `Real-world anchor` and `Reference window` metadata fields. The era is anchored on a representative Early Pleistocene glacial maximum. The Early Pleistocene runs from approximately 2.58 MYA to ~0.77 MYA, with ~40,000-year glacial cycles in its earlier phase. The 2 MYA anchor places Protohistoric Expansion well within Early Pleistocene glacial dynamics. Uncertainty around the anchor is narrow at the geological level (the snapshot is intentionally a representative cycle, not a specific event).

### Era-by-era calendar mapping

The table below gives approximate ranges for each era. Each range carries explicit uncertainty bounds. Reasoning for each placement follows the table.

| Era | Approximate range | Width | Confidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Claw Development Era | ~12 MYA – ~7 MYA | ~5 million years | Low (wide range) |
| Fire and Extended Development Era | ~7 MYA – ~3 MYA | ~4 million years | Low (wide range) |
| Ecological Management Era | ~3 MYA – ~2.4 MYA | ~600,000 years | Medium |
| Orchard Era | ~2.4 MYA – ~2.15 MYA | ~250,000 years | Medium |
| Kati Thunda Ant Revolution | ~2.15 MYA – ~2.10 MYA | ~50,000 years | Medium-high (threshold event) |
| Protohistoric Expansion Era | ~2.10 MYA – ~2.0 MYA (canonical ~2 MYA) | ~100,000 years | High (anchored) |

The full named-era span runs from ~12 MYA to ~1.7 MYA, totalling approximately 10.3 million years. The biological eras account for ~9 million years of that span. The civilisational eras account for ~700,000 years.

### Reasoning for placements

**Claw Development Era (~12 MYA – ~7 MYA).** Anchored at ~12 MYA by the divergence doc. The era covers the deep biological substrate: chick wing-claw clasping under predator pressure, father-assisted carrying, the slow accumulation of forelimb anatomy that later supports manipulation. Stages 1–2 of the cassowary forelimb manipulation pathway span this era. The era ends when the wing-claw system has stabilised enough that downstream behaviours (egg relocation, low climbing, proto-tool use) begin to be selected for. The placement is wide because there is no biological or palaeontological anchor between the divergence onset and the start of the next era.

**Fire and Extended Development Era (~7 MYA – ~3 MYA).** Begins where Claw Development hands off. Spans the bulk of the cassowary forelimb manipulation pathway (stages 3–11) — egg relocation, simple object handling, low climbing, food processing, insect extraction, cockatoo guidance partnership, diet broadening, fire following and fire use, managed incubation and extended juvenile development. Also spans the early phases of cockatoo–cassowary signal coevolution (stages 1–6, including functional mimicry and cassowary-only reuse). Ends when extended juvenile development, fire use, and operational signal language are stable enough for ecological partnerships at landscape scale. The wide range reflects how much biological and behavioural change must accumulate; the end-point is calibrated to allow the next eras to fit within the residual ~3 million years before the Protohistoric Expansion anchor.

**Ecological Management Era (~3 MYA – ~2.4 MYA).** Begins when cassowary signal language and manipulation are sufficient to support deliberate ecological work — aphid stabilisation, orchard ant management, sap-feeder cultivation, regional ecotype differentiation. Spans stages 6–9 of cockatoo–cassowary signal coevolution (signal simplification, generalisation, embodied proto-language, institutional extension). The era is shorter than the deep biological eras because the ecological practices are downstream of biology that is now in place. Uncertainty is medium because the boundary with the next era (Orchard) is genuinely ambiguous in the existing canon (see Open Questions below).

**Orchard Era (~2.4 MYA – ~2.15 MYA).** Begins when cassowary investment in orchard trees becomes multigenerational — when planting and propagating tree lineages with decades-long maturation timescales becomes a viable strategy. Graft cuttings become political alliance. Boom-bust population dynamics persist because durable storage does not yet exist. The era is short relative to the ecological substrate because intentional orchard investment is a cultural transition rather than an evolutionary one.

**Kati Thunda Ant Revolution (~2.15 MYA – ~2.10 MYA).** A threshold event, not a long era. The world-state doc describes it as the moment when pottery-assisted honeypot ant storage becomes reliable enough to carry calories across the lean season at scale. The transition spans roughly one to two glacial cycles (~40,000–80,000 years) — enough time for the practice to stabilise regionally and for the political economy to begin shifting from access-control to storage-control. Confidence is medium-high because the threshold is defined by the technological capability rather than by an arbitrary period.

**Protohistoric Expansion Era (~2.10 MYA – ~2.0 MYA, canonical snapshot ~2 MYA).** Anchored. The world-state doc places this era at a representative Early Pleistocene glacial maximum. Spanning roughly two to three glacial cycles around ~2 MYA, the era contains the emergence of sealed-vessel tribute, regional expansion, and proto-WTA trade relationships. The narrow span reflects the high-confidence anchor.

**Early History Era (~2.0 MYA – ~1.90 MYA).** Begins where Protohistoric Expansion stabilises. The world-state doc characterises this era as the first in which institutional infrastructure — routes, redistribution centres, tribute systems, handler lineages, political hierarchies — is durable enough to be treated as background rather than recent innovation. Spans roughly two to three glacial cycles. Ends when the structural fragility documented in the Collapse Era doc becomes critical.

**Collapse Era (~1.90 MYA – ~1.85 MYA).** A short, intense window of cascading institutional failure. Diprotodontid working lineage fragility, water-stop dependency, and centralised storage become brittle simultaneously across multiple regions. The era's brevity reflects the speed at which cascading failures can propagate once a critical infrastructure element is lost. Recovery is partial and uneven; full institutional reorganisation begins after the era.

**Logistics Stabilisation Era / WTA Period (~1.85 MYA – ~1.70 MYA).** Begins as post-collapse reorganisation. The world-state doc is explicit that WTA is a post-collapse logistics architecture — distributed nodes, route redundancy, institutional delivery guarantees — built in conscious response to the Collapse Era's failure modes. The era is wider than the Collapse Era because institutional rebuilding takes longer than institutional failure. The end-point is left open in current canon; this document treats ~1.70 MYA as a notional close while flagging it as low-confidence.

### Two specific sub-anchor relationships

These are clarifications inside the table above. They matter for cross-doc consistency.

- **Orchard Era precedes Kati Thunda Ant Revolution, which precedes Protohistoric Expansion Era.** This sequence is established by the `What Changed from...` sections of the respective world-state docs. Orchard Era has no durable storage; Kati Thunda crosses the storage threshold; Protohistoric Expansion has sealed-vessel tribute operating.
- **WTA Period / Logistics Stabilisation Era follows Collapse Era, not Early History Era directly.** This contradicts a naïve reading of "WTA-style trade" mentioned in the Early History Era doc. The Logistics Stabilisation Era doc clarifies: WTA-style trade in the Early History Era is proto-WTA; the formal WTA framework with institutional delivery guarantees is the post-collapse reform. This document treats the two as different institutional phases of the same general framework, with the Logistics Stabilisation Era being the canonical "WTA Period."

## Constraints

- Only the two hard anchors (~12 MYA divergence onset, ~2 MYA Protohistoric Expansion canonical snapshot) are treated as fixed. Every other date in this document is a tentative calibration.
- The widths given in the mapping table are the ranges within which each era plausibly falls; they are not minimum or maximum durations of the era itself. A real era could be narrower than its given range or wider, within the explicitly noted confidence level.
- The civilisational eras (Orchard through WTA Period) are clustered close to the Protohistoric Expansion anchor because the alternative — distributing civilisational change across millions of years — contradicts the evidence base of how fast institutional change actually occurs in real societies.
- The biological eras (Claw Development, Fire and Extended Development, Ecological Management) are deep because the evolutionary and behavioural changes they document require deep time to accumulate. Compressing them contradicts the gradualist constraint in `docs/guiding-principles.md`.
- Dates given in this document must not be cited as direct fossil observations or as molecular-clock estimates. They are derived placements calibrated against the two hard anchors and the biological/civilisational requirements of each era.
- Specific year-counts (e.g. "~2.15 MYA") are illustrative midpoints of ranges. The range, not the midpoint, is the canonical claim.
- Boundaries between adjacent eras are transitions, not events. Where one era is said to end at ~2.4 MYA and the next to begin there, the transition spans some number of generations across that approximate point.

## System Implications

- Cross-doc consistency checks for sequence and duration claims can now be performed against this document. Where a lore doc says "much later than" or "decades after," the question is whether the time-period metadata is consistent with the ranges established here.
- Open Questions in the four restructured era docs that asked for absolute calendar mapping should be supplemented (not removed) with cross-reference to this document's canonical ranges. The questions remain open because the ranges are tentative; this document does not pretend to resolve them.
- Future regional differentiation work (see Cluster 10 in the project audit) can now anchor regional timelines to a shared absolute baseline. Regions reaching Early History Era earlier or later can be specified in calendar years rather than only in qualitative comparatives.
- Story-pipeline work can now use approximate dates when needed for narrative scale, while explicitly preserving the uncertainty that this document records.
- Any future revision of these ranges should update this document first, then propagate to dependent docs through their metadata.

## Known Variability

- The Late Miocene to Early Pliocene boundary (~5 MYA) sits roughly inside the Fire and Extended Development Era. Climate-driven ecological shifts at that boundary may have accelerated or constrained specific behavioural transitions; the era is treated as continuous through the boundary in this document.
- The Pliocene to Pleistocene boundary (~2.58 MYA) sits inside the Ecological Management Era. This boundary involved major global cooling and the establishment of Northern Hemisphere ice sheets; Sahul-region effects were less dramatic but real. The era is treated as continuous.
- Pleistocene glacial-interglacial cycles (~40,000-year cycles in the early Pleistocene) sit within and across the civilisational eras. Specific eras may align with particular glacial maxima or interglacial intervals; the world-state docs use representative glacial maxima as their snapshot baselines.
- Regions of Sahul reached different eras at different times. The dates given here are reference baselines for the lineages and regions that reached each era first or most fully. Other regions may have lagged by tens of thousands of years or more.
- The mapping presented here is built from current canon. If future canonical revisions move the wing-claw divergence date or the Protohistoric Expansion anchor, every other range in this document should be recalibrated.

## Open Questions

- How precise can the Claw Development Era onset date be made against current palaeontological evidence for marsupial-predator diversification in Sahul? Is the ~12 MYA estimate tied to a specific predator-fossil window, or is it more loosely calibrated against general Late Miocene ecological conditions?
- How long does the Fire and Extended Development Era actually need to be, given the stages of the cassowary forelimb manipulation pathway and the cockatoo–cassowary signal coevolution that span it? Is the ~4 million year range adequate, generous, or tight?
- Is Ecological Management Era genuinely after Fire and Extended Development Era, or does it begin during it as a regional or partial development before stabilising universally? The era ordering convention in this document treats them as sequential; this may oversimplify.
- Where exactly does Ecological Management Era end and Orchard Era begin, and what evidence (or set of canonical claims) distinguishes them? The current placement is calibrated by the Orchard Era's "What Changed from the Fire and Extended Development Era" section but does not resolve the relationship to Ecological Management.
- How long was the Kati Thunda Ant Revolution as a transition window? The ~50,000-year placement is illustrative; the threshold could be sharper (a few generations) or broader (multiple glacial cycles) depending on how the storage technology actually crossed reliability thresholds.
- What is the calendar separation between First New Zealand Arrival and Sahul-NZ Maritime Contact, given that both currently sit within the Protohistoric Expansion Era? The era is approximately 100,000 years wide in this document; how is that span partitioned between the two events?
- What is the canonical end of the Logistics Stabilisation Era / WTA Period? This document places it at ~1.70 MYA notionally; later events in Cassowary World history are not yet documented, so this end-point is provisional.
- Should this document be revised when regional differentiation lore is added, to provide per-region calendar anchors alongside the Sahul-wide canonical placements?
