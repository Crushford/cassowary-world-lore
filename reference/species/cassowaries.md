# Cassowaries

Layer: Real-world reference
Status: Draft

## Summary

This reference explains real-world cassowary traits that matter for Cassowary World divergence and system design.

## Useful Baseline

- Cassowaries are large, ground-dwelling, flightless birds.
- Cassowaries have strong legs and are adapted for movement through dense vegetation.
- Real cassowaries do not have human-like manipulative hands.
- Cassowary social and reproductive patterns include strong female territoriality and male parental care.
- Cassowary anatomy constrains climbing, carrying, heat management, architecture, transport, and tool use in Cassowary World.

## Relevance to Cassowary World

Cassowary World begins from this biological baseline and then introduces a controlled wing-claw divergence. Later civilization must still follow cassowary anatomy rather than importing human assumptions.
