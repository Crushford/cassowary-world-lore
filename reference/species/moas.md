# Moas

Layer: Real-world reference
Status: Draft

## Summary

This reference documents real-world moas as a baseline for Cassowary World lore. It covers their biology, ecology, and relevant constraints. Domestication, ridability, and Cassowary World interactions are lore, not reference baseline.

## Useful Baseline

- Moas were a group of extinct flightless birds native to New Zealand.
- Moas were ratites, part of the same broad lineage as ostriches, emus, cassowaries, rheas, and kiwis.
- Moas had no functional wings. Unlike other ratites, they had no wing bones at all or only vestigial remnants.
- Moa species varied widely in size, from turkey-sized forms to the giant South Island giant moa (*Dinornis robustus*).
- Some moa lineages showed strong sexual size dimorphism, with females substantially larger than males. In some species, females were roughly twice the body mass of males.
- Moas were herbivorous browsers, feeding on leaves, fruit, seeds, and fibrous plant material across a range of New Zealand habitats.
- Male parental care is plausible in moa lineages given ratite-wide patterns, but should be treated cautiously and verified independently before use in detailed lore arguments.
- Haast's eagle (*Hieraaetus moorei*) was a major predator of moas and the primary predator-pressure baseline for moa behaviour.
- In the real world, moas were driven to extinction rapidly following human arrival in New Zealand, likely within one to two centuries of first settlement.
- No moa population survived into the historical period.

## Limits

- Do not treat moas as naturally rideable or horse-like herd animals. Domestication and ridability are Cassowary World lore dependent on specific mechanisms.
- Do not infer flight capability, wing use, or upper limb manipulation from moas. They had no functional wings.
- Do not assume uniform behaviour across all moa species. Size, habitat, and life history varied significantly.
- Do not import human domestication timelines or livestock assumptions into moa handling.

## Relevance to Cassowary World

Larger female moas are the primary riding candidates in Cassowary World, based on body size. This is an inference for lore development, not a claim established in this reference document. The real-world extinction pattern under human predation supports caution about assuming easy tameability. Domestication requires a specific mechanism documented in lore.
