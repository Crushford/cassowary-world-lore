# Ceramic Vessels and Nest Chambers

Layer: Real-world reference
Status: Draft

## Summary

This reference establishes the real-world material baseline for fired clay vessels as shaped, durable containers and chambers. It defines why ceramic cavities are plausible technical components in managed biological systems without claiming that real-world honeypot ant pottery farming exists.

## Useful Baseline

- Fired clay vessels are durable, shapeable, and can create enclosed cavities with controlled access, stable walls, and reasonable resistance to moisture and microbial intrusion.
- Vessel shape affects access (narrow necks restrict entry), stability (wide bases resist tipping), airflow (perforations or gaps allow passive ventilation), moisture retention or exclusion (wall thickness and firing temperature affect permeability), and portability (size and handle position affect carrying).
- Small ceramic chambers can be placed into or adjacent to living biological systems to provide protected, dry, stable cavity volume. The chamber does not need to be large to be functionally useful; a small dry pocket near a nest entrance can change how the colony uses the surrounding space.
- Larger sealed jars can store processed food separately from any living system. Once contents are sealed, the vessel is dead-storage infrastructure rather than a living-system component.
- Ceramic forms that are useful for nest assistance and those useful for tribute storage have different shape requirements. Nest-assist chambers need rough exteriors, stable placement, and repeated access; tribute jars need smooth or marked exteriors, reliable seals, and standardised volume.
- This reference does not claim that real-world honeypot ant pottery farming exists or has been documented. It defines why ceramic cavities are plausible technical components given the documented properties of fired clay.

## Cassowary World Implications

Pottery-assisted nest management is a Cassowary World innovation enabled by cassowary manipulation capacity, ceramic technology, and accumulated ecological knowledge. The reference baseline establishes that ceramic chambers are materially capable of doing what the lore claims; it does not assert the practice independently of cassowary agency.

The distinction between nest-assist chambers and sealed storage vessels is important. They have different shapes, functions, sizes, and social meanings. Do not conflate them.
