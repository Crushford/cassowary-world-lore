# Transport Networks

Status: Draft

## Summary

This placeholder technical document will define routes, path maintenance, transport infrastructure, and movement patterns that support trade, tribute, and regional coordination.

It exists because preserved surplus and political integration imply repeatable movement systems.

## Definition

Transport networks are the maintained routes, nodes, and movement protocols used to move goods, people, and information between settlements and productive landscapes.

## Constraints and Limits

- Terrain, weather, and seasonal flooding alter route reliability.
- Route maintenance requires labor coordination and political incentives.
- Transport capacity may constrain trade scale even when surplus exists.
- Predator and conflict risks affect route choice and escort needs.

## Civilizational Implications

- Enables redistribution of preserved surplus beyond local production zones.
- Supports political coordination across dispersed settlements.
- Creates strategic control points and potential taxation chokepoints.

## Depends On

- settlement nodes worth connecting
- labor coordination for route maintenance
- stored goods or information worth moving at scale
