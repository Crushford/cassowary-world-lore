# Storage and Ceramic Technology

Status: Draft

## Summary

This placeholder technical document will define the storage-container and ceramic infrastructure required for large-scale preservation, sealing, measurement, and transport of surplus foods (including sugar concentrates).

It exists because preservation and taxation claims imply durable containers, standardized vessels, and sealing practices.

## Definition

Storage and ceramic technology is the set of material practices used to shape, fire, seal, standardize, and reuse containers for food preservation and administrative storage.

## Timeline Placement

- Pottery-assisted ecological use near ant nests: ~4-3 MYA (provisional, implied by honeypot-ant canon candidate; requires dedicated validation)
- Large-scale preserved surplus storage: Unknown — requires future technical document.

## Constraints and Limits

- Ceramic production depends on suitable clays, fuel, labor time, and skill.
- Breakage, firing inconsistency, and sealing failure reduce storage reliability.
- Standardization may emerge later than basic container production.
- Transport demands may require container forms different from local storage forms.

## Civilizational Implications

- Converts biological or perishable surplus into administratively storable units.
- Enables sealable tribute containers and redistributable rations.
- Supports specialization by extending food durability and transportability.

## Depends On

- material access (clay, fuel, sealants)
- tool use and repeated craft practice
- preservation needs created by orchard and sugar systems
