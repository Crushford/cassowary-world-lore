# Sahul Food Forest Functional Crop Portfolio

Status: Draft

## Summary

This technical document defines a draft crop strategy for cassowary civilization expansion using a **functional food-forest portfolio** rather than a single staple species.

The core idea is to scale agriculture north-south across Sahul by preserving the same crop roles (base calories, oil wealth, buffers, specialist species) while swapping species within each role to match local ecology.

## Definition

The "functional crop portfolio" model is a classification system for orchard and forest-managed crops based on civilizational function:

- calorie reliability
- oil production
- grafting suitability
- geographic adaptability
- cultural exclusivity or species lock-in

This model supports a distributed orchard civilization instead of a grain monoculture.

## Candidate Crop Groups (Draft)

All entries below are draft candidates pending technical validation, regional ecology mapping, and timeline fit.

### High-oil / nut analog fruits

#### Macadamia (*Macadamia integrifolia / tetraphylla* complex)

- Grafting suitability: Excellent within genus
- Caloric density: Extremely high, ~700+ kcal per 100 g kernel
- Oil quality: Outstanding, ~70-75% fat, stable monounsaturated oil
- Wild range: SE Queensland to NE New South Wales
- Potential range in cooler Sahul: Subtropical east coast, frost-free river corridors, sheltered uplands
- Notes: Best pure oil tree in Australia. Direct olive analogue. Slow maturity but very high long-term yield.

#### Blue quandong group (*Elaeocarpus* spp.)

- Grafting suitability: Good within genus
- Caloric density: Moderate flesh, high potential in seed oils
- Oil quality: Variable, some species oil rich
- Wild range: QLD rainforests through NSW
- Potential range: Broad east coast gradient with regional species swaps
- Notes: Structurally ideal for selection into oil drupes. Already bird-dispersal optimized.

#### Native palms (*Archontophoenix*, *Livistona*, etc.)

- Grafting suitability: Poor to impossible (true palms rarely graft)
- Caloric density: Moderate to high depending on species
- Oil quality: Some species fatty, fiber heavy
- Wild range: Tropical to subtropical east coast, northern Australia
- Potential range: Northern Sahul, river valleys
- Notes: Managed by seed and transplant, not grafting. Fast growing calorie buffer layer.

### High sugar / staple reliability fruits

#### Figs (*Ficus* spp.)

- Grafting suitability: Excellent within genus
- Caloric density: Moderate sugar dense fruit
- Oil quality: Low
- Wild range: Entire east coast from tropics to warm temperate zones
- Potential range: Very broad, especially along water and cliffs
- Notes: Keystone famine buffer. Best candidate for year-round fruit selection.

#### Lilly pilly complex (*Syzygium* spp.)

- Grafting suitability: Good within genus
- Caloric density: Moderate
- Oil quality: Low
- Wild range: Tropical QLD to Victoria in pockets
- Potential range: Wide east coast adaptability
- Notes: Extremely flexible genus. Ideal for north-south cultural crop continuity.

#### Davidson's plum (*Davidsonia* spp.)

- Grafting suitability: Moderate to good within genus
- Caloric density: Moderate, flesh heavy
- Oil quality: Low to moderate potential
- Wild range: Eastern rainforests QLD-NSW
- Potential range: Subtropical to warm temperate forest
- Notes: Large fruit, strong candidate for sweetness selection.

### Toxic-to-humans but cassowary-specialist drupes

#### Cassowary plum (*Cerbera floribunda*)

- Grafting suitability: Good within genus
- Caloric density: Moderate flesh
- Oil quality: Possible seed oil potential
- Wild range: Coastal QLD-NSW rainforests
- Potential range: Frost-free coastal belt
- Notes: Flesh edible to cassowaries, toxic to humans. Strong species lock-in advantage for cassowary culture.

### Oil-sugar hybrid candidates

#### Laurel / nutmeg relatives (various Lauraceae, Myristicaceae)

- Grafting suitability: Moderate within family clusters
- Caloric density: Moderate
- Oil quality: Aromatic, fatty flesh
- Wild range: Northern QLD + New Guinea rainforests
- Potential range: Northern Sahul uplands and wet corridors
- Notes: Natural olive-analogue ecology. Ritual oil potential.

## Civilizational Layering Model (Draft)

If these crop groups are stacked ecologically, the proposed civilizational food forest looks like:

- Base calorie layer: figs + lilly pillies
- Oil wealth layer: macadamia + laurel drupes
- Regional identity fruits: plums + quandongs
- Fast buffer crops: palms
- Cassowary-exclusive crops: *Cerbera* lineages

This creates a food forest system that scales north-south by swapping species inside each functional category rather than forcing one fragile crop across all climates.

The civilization spreads by copying **roles**, not species.

## Mechanism / Process (Why This Scales)

The model addresses expansion and continuity in a multi-climate landscape through:

1. Functional redundancy (multiple species can fill similar roles).
2. Regional substitution (species swaps preserve system behavior across climate gradients).
3. Mixed propagation methods (grafting where possible, seed/transplant where necessary).
4. Layered risk management (calorie base, oil wealth, buffers, and specialist crops coexist).
5. Cultural standardization by role (administration can tax/plan by crop class even when species differ).

This is a potential workaround to latitude-related crop transfer limits: the civilization replicates an ecological design pattern, not a single crop package.

## Constraints and Limits

- Species claims require validation against actual cassowary ranges, ecology, and timeline assumptions
- Grafting suitability does not guarantee large-scale domestication success
- High caloric density does not automatically imply high yield under pre-industrial management
- Toxicity and cassowary tolerance claims must be treated as technical-canon hypotheses until confirmed
- Regional climate shifts (including glacial/interglacial periods) may change suitability ranges
- This portfolio does not replace the need for preservation and storage systems; it complements them

## Implications for Civilization and Story

- Supports a distinctive orchard-empire model instead of a grain-state template
- Enables regional diversity without breaking civilizational continuity
- Creates oil, sugar, and famine-buffer classes that can map to tribute systems and trade
- Introduces strategic "species lock-in" crops that reinforce cassowary political advantage
- Makes ecological knowledge, grafting practice, and orchard management central to state capacity
