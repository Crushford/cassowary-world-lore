This placeholder technical document will define how surplus collection, measurement, storage rights, and redistribution become institutionalized in cassowary societies.

## Definition

Tribute and taxation are institutional systems for recurring surplus collection and redistribution, including rules for obligation, measurement, storage, custody, and enforcement.

## Constraints and Limits

- Taxation cannot scale without measurable surplus and storage reliability.
- Collection systems depend on route access and transport capacity.
- Overextraction can collapse productive ecological systems and political legitimacy.
- Enforcement costs may exceed returns in dispersed landscapes.

## Civilizational Implications

- Supports political centralization and administrative specialization.
- Converts ecological surplus into durable state capacity.
- Creates conflict over custody, ownership, and distribution rules.
