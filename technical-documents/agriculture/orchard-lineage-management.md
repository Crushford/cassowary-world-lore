# Orchard Lineage Management

Status: Draft

## Summary

This placeholder technical document will define how cassowaries select, propagate, maintain, and regionally adapt orchard tree lineages over multiple generations.

It exists to anchor dependencies implied by honeypot-ant production, crop portfolio planning, and long-term orchard stability claims.

## Definition

Orchard lineage management is the set of practices used to preserve and improve productive tree lineages over time (for example through selection, propagation, grafting, replacement, and orchard succession planning).

## Constraints and Limits

- Tree maturation times create long feedback cycles.
- Regional climates and soils may restrict lineage transfer.
- Propagation success is likely species-dependent.
- Labor and land tenure constraints may limit long-term orchard planning.

## Civilizational Implications

- Increases reliability of orchard-based calorie and sugar systems.
- Supports long-lived productive landscapes needed for stable settlements.
- Enables regional adaptation without losing functional crop roles.

## Depends On

- cassowary manipulation capacity and tool use sufficient for propagation work
- ecological knowledge of local tree species and site conditions
- social continuity across generations (for lineage memory)
