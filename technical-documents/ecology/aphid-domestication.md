# Aphid Domestication

Status: Draft

## Summary

This placeholder technical document will define how cassowaries stabilize, manage, or selectively favor aphid populations as part of the orchard -> honeydew -> ant sugar production chain.

It exists because honeypot-ant domestication implies a distinct ecological management layer between host trees and ant reserves.

## Definition

Aphid domestication (or aphid stabilization) is the deliberate ecological management of aphid-host relationships to increase predictable honeydew production without collapsing host tree health.

## Constraints and Limits

- Aphid overgrowth can damage host trees and reduce long-term productivity.
- Predator and parasite pressure may destabilize managed colonies.
- Seasonal and climate variation can sharply alter yields.
- Management likely depends on orchard continuity and observation labor.

## Civilizational Implications

- Increases reliability of honeypot-ant sugar production.
- Makes ecological pest management a core productive skill.
- Creates an intermediate dependency between orchard management and preserved surplus.
