# Tribute Seal Integrity and Authority Marks

## Summary

This document answers how sealed tribute vessels carry trust and authority before writing. It focuses on tamper evidence, seal materials, custody marks, and recognized authority impressions.

## Metadata

- Primary topic: tribute seal integrity and authority marks
- Layer: Cassowary World system
- Topics: tribute, seal marks, custody, tamper evidence, authority symbols
- Era: Protohistoric Expansion Era
- Regions: some core regions of Sahul
- Related technologies: resin seals, wax seals, clay seals, ceramic lids, stamp objects
- Related institutions: tribute, taxation, storage custody

## Atomic Notes

- A tribute vessel enters custody when it is filled to a recognized standard, closed, sealed, and visibly marked.
- Closure materials and sealants may vary by region and period, but a trustworthy seal must visibly bind the vessel opening.
- Opening or tampering with a sealed tribute vessel destroys or visibly disturbs the seal.
- Authority marks impressed into outer seal material encode household, collection-node, storehouse, elite, or ritual custody without requiring writing.
- Custody transitions can be witnessed through new seal impressions, allowing a jar to display custody history in visible layers.
- Marks may be pressed with claws, carved seal tools, or prepared stamp objects.
- Seal marks distinguish collector and storehouse custody during standardized sealed vessel tribute.
- Seal trust depends on material durability under heat, humidity, storage conditions, and repeated handling.
- False marks or imitation seals may emerge once tribute gains value.
- Seal integrity becomes a state-capacity multiplier because it lets institutions trust stored goods after they leave producer control.

## Context

Seals make tribute legible after it leaves the producer. A vessel without a trustworthy seal is only a container; a sealed vessel can move between households, collection nodes, and storehouses while still showing whether it has been opened.

Authority marks also let custody accumulate in visible layers. A household mark, collection mark, and storehouse mark can all matter without requiring writing. This system is therefore a bridge between physical tribute units and later administrative practice.

Seal integrity depends on [Tribute Sealed Vessel Units](tribute-sealed-vessel-units.md). It supports [Tribute Collection Counting and Enforcement](tribute-collection-counting-and-enforcement.md) because counters and witnesses need visible seal status to inspect obligations.
