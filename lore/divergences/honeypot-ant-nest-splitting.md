# Honeypot Ant Nest Splitting

## Summary

This divergence file tracks the Cassowary World change that makes one domesticated honeypot ant lineage more scalable than ordinary real-world honeypot ant behavior.

## Metadata

- Layer: Fundamental divergence
- Primary topic: honeypot ant nest splitting
- Topics: honeypot ants, domestication, nest propagation, scalable sugar production
- Time periods: `~3-2.5 MYA`
- Status: Canon
- Related species: honeypot ants, cassowaries

## What Changes

One domesticated honeypot ant lineage can split and propagate nests in a way that is not ordinary real-world honeypot ant behavior.

## When

This divergence is relevant by `~3-2.5 MYA`, when managed ant production needs a scalable propagation path beyond local colony protection.

## Immediate Effect

- Managed honeypot ant production becomes more scalable than protecting only existing wild colonies.
- Regional expansion of ant-based sugar systems can rely on colony replication instead of only colony defense.
- Lore that depends on colony propagation should reference this divergence rather than assuming baseline behavior.

## Systems Depending On It

- Honeypot ant orchard expansion where new managed colonies must be propagated.
- Large-scale ant-derived sugar production beyond existing wild colony distribution.
- Regional surplus systems that rely on replication of managed colonies rather than only protection of local nests.

## Open Questions

- What biological mechanism makes nest splitting viable in this lineage?
- Does nest splitting occur naturally first, or only under cassowary management?
