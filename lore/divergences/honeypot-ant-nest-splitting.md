# Honeypot Ant Nest Splitting

## Summary

This divergence file tracks the Cassowary World change that makes one domesticated honeypot ant lineage more scalable than ordinary real-world honeypot ant behavior.

## Metadata

- Layer: Fundamental divergence
- Primary topic: honeypot ant nest splitting
- Topics: honeypot ants, domestication, nest propagation, scalable sugar production

- Related species: honeypot ants, cassowaries
- Source documents: [Honeypot Ant Domestication and Sugar Preservation](../../technical-documents/ecology/honeypot-ant-domestication-and-sugar-preservation.md)

## What Changes

One domesticated honeypot ant lineage can have nests split and propagated in a way loosely analogous to beehive splitting.

This is not treated as ordinary real-world honeypot ant behavior.

## When

No canonical time label is assigned yet. The divergence matters to systems that need scalable propagation beyond local colony protection, especially once early domestication and institutionalized surplus become relevant.

## Capabilities Enabled

- Nest splitting makes managed honeypot ant production more scalable than simple protection of existing wild colonies.
- Scalable propagation helps explain regional expansion of ant-based sugar systems.
- This divergence should be referenced when lore claims depend on colony propagation rather than local colony management.

## Systems Depending On It

- Honeypot ant orchard expansion where new managed colonies must be propagated.
- Large-scale ant-derived sugar production beyond existing wild colony distribution.
- Regional surplus systems that rely on replication of managed colonies rather than only protection of local nests.
