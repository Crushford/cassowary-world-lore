# First New Zealand Arrival

## Summary

This document establishes that cassowaries reached New Zealand before the regular Sahul-New Zealand maritime trade routes developed. It locks in the fact of early arrival while leaving the mechanism unresolved. First arrival does not imply mature maritime trade.

## Atomic Notes

- Cassowary presence in New Zealand begins before regular Sahul-New Zealand maritime trade routes.
- First arrival occurs thousands of years or more before the later moa trade system.
- The exact mechanism of first arrival is unresolved.
- Plausible models include storm drift, accidental displacement from coastal routes, rare exploratory voyaging, watercraft failure, or some combination of these.
- No single mechanism is confirmed. The question remains open.
- First arrival does not imply mature open-ocean navigation.
- First arrival does not imply broad cassowary expansion outside the Sahul-New Zealand system.
- Early New Zealand cassowary groups become isolated long enough to develop local lifeways and moa-handling traditions before reconnection.
- Later trade routes connect Sahul societies to already-established New Zealand cassowary groups and managed moa lineages, not to an uninhabited island.

## Context

The canon claim in [Moa Mounted Warfare](moa-mounted-warfare.md) requires that the northern empire can import moas from New Zealand as an already-working system. Working backwards, someone had to establish moa domestication in New Zealand first. Working further back, cassowaries had to reach New Zealand before any moa handling could begin.

This document establishes that prior presence without needing to resolve the arrival mechanism. The isolation period between first arrival and later regular trade is important: it gives New Zealand cassowary communities time to develop local moa-handling traditions independently, so that when Sahul contact resumes, there is already an established system to access.

## Limits

- Do not infer a durable blue-water navigation tradition from first arrival. The mechanism may have been accidental.
- Do not infer broad geographic expansion from first arrival. The Sahul-New Zealand route is the only system supported here.
- Do not shorten the isolation period speculatively. The gap between arrival and regular trade needs to be long enough for independent moa domestication traditions to develop.

## Open Questions

- Which glacial maximum or low-sea-level window best fits the first arrival timing?
- Was the arrival accidental, exploratory, storm-driven, or mixed?
- How large was the founding cassowary population?
- Did early settlers retain memory of Sahul, or did Sahul become mythic before reconnection?
- Why did early maritime capability not produce durable expansion beyond the Sahul-New Zealand system?
