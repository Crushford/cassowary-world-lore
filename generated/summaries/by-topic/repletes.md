## Key Atomic Notes

### Honeypot Ant Harvesting

- Cassowaries make ant reserves harvestable by placing clay vessels or ceramic chambers near colony entrances and likely expansion zones.
- Artificial chambers encourage nest expansion when they provide stable, protected, dry volume.
- Pottery-assisted localization concentrates repletes into predictable access points without creating the replete-storage trait.
- In `4-3 MYA`, regional pottery-assisted proto-domestication shifts from opportunistic vessel placement toward intentional biasing of nest architecture.
- Orchard management and colony management integrate as harvesters plan ant storage around productive host-tree systems.
- Sustainable harvest removes only part of a colony's reserve capacity while preserving seasonal recovery.
- Harvest methods may remove accessible repletes or vessel-contained storage clusters, then replace prepared chambers to encourage reoccupation.
- Colonies are difficult to relocate long distances; claims about scalable colony propagation should reference the nest-splitting divergence.
- Overharvest and political extraction pressure can reduce colony resilience or cause local collapse.
