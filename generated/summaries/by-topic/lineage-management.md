## Key Atomic Notes

### Orchard Sugar Tree Lineages

- Tree choice controls honeydew flow. The choice of host tree is the upstream variable in the whole orchard-ant-tribute chain.
- Stable flow matters more than maximum flow. A tree that supports predictable moderate sap-feeder populations is more valuable than one that allows a spike and then declines.
- Aphid and sap-feeder overgrowth can damage and kill host trees. Managers must balance infestation density against tree health.
- Regional substitutions can preserve orchard function even when exact species differ. The functional role is what matters; the species name is secondary.
- Grafting and propagation preserve functional traits across generations when seed propagation does not reliably pass on those traits.
- Tree lineages become technical and possibly institutional knowledge. Knowing which trees to propagate, how, and where is expert knowledge with economic and political value.
- Long tree maturation cycles mean orchard investment is multigenerational. Trees planted in one generation may reach full productivity in the next.
- Tree loss — from disease, climate, over-infestation, or political disruption — is a long-cycle setback, not a short-cycle problem.
