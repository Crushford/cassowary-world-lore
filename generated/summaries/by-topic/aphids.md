## Key Atomic Notes

### Aphid Stabilization

- Aphid stabilization is deliberate ecological management of host-tree and aphid relationships, not a new biological invention.
- Cassowaries increase honeydew reliability by keeping productive aphid populations within the range that host trees can sustain.
- The management target is stable flow, not maximal aphid abundance.
- Effective stabilization depends on host-tree choice, disturbance control, predator suppression where feasible, and seasonal recovery cycles.
- Aphid overgrowth can damage host trees and reduce long-term orchard productivity.
- Predator and parasite pressure can destabilize managed aphid populations.
- Climate shifts can break aphid-host stability and cascade into lower honeydew flow for honeypot ant systems.
- Aphid stabilization is an intermediate dependency between orchard lineage management and honeypot-ant reserve production.
